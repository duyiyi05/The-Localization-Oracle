When Translation Is Not Enough

A localization audit of Agoda's Turkish website: cultural adaptation, UX redesign, and trust architecture for a market that needed more than words.

The Brief

The Platform Was Translated. Not Localized.

A global travel platform with millions of users had a Turkish website. The homepage ran a literal rendering of its English tagline. Discount banners displayed prices in euros. CTAs used awkward placeholder phrasing. The checkout flow mixed English and Turkish mid-sentence. Technically available in Turkish, it did not feel Turkish.

"Surface-level translation had masked a deeper structural issue: the platform's entire UX logic was built for a Western user profile."

The Problem

Five Problems. One Root Cause.

The audit identified issues across language and tone, visual hierarchy, trust signals, payment UX, and SEO. Each was a symptom of the same decision: treating Turkish as a translation target rather than a distinct market.

The Judgment Call

Strategic Decisions, Not Translation Fixes

The easy path was a copy audit: fix the mistranslations, replace the euro signs, shorten the CTAs. I chose the harder path — making the case that the problem was strategic. These were product decisions, not translation decisions. I framed them as product decisions, with evidence.

🔑
Trust Architecture
Taksit, ücretsiz iptal, and TRY pricing are not cosmetic additions. They are the structural trust signals Turkish users scan for before they consider anything else. Putting them above the fold is a product decision.
🗺
Domestic First
Surfacing Antalya, Istanbul, and Kapadokya before Bangkok and Paris is not about limiting options. It is about signalling that this platform understands who is using it. Familiarity precedes trust.
🔍
SEO Mismatch
Turkish users search "ucuz oteller" and "Antalya otelleri" — not "cheap hotels" or "best hotels Turkey." The existing metadata was optimised for an English-speaking audience that was not there.
🧠
Cultural Register
High uncertainty avoidance means urgency tactics ("Book now! Only 2 left!") create anxiety, not conversion. Warmth, clarity, and visible safety nets outperform pressure in this market.
